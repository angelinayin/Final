PJ’s, check. Drink on hand, check. Camera on, check. Our beloved Zoom is up and running.
Over the past few months, Zoom has delighted everyone from elementary school-age kids,
to college-age kids, to kids-at-heart adults.
If you were to head to your Google search engine and type in “how many people use Zoom,”
you’ll see a number as high as 12 million staring back.


But even beyond the hefty number of Zoom’s users,
is its deep importance to social connectivity.
Zoom is “fast becoming the world's most popular new social network,”
Jeff Parsons wrote in a Metro article.


While businesses and schools have turned to video conferencing
for remote work and distant-learning, families and individuals
have also turned to the face-to-face substitute. In this self-isolating time,
the Zoom conferencing platform has become the center to many's quarantining life,
hosting events including virtual gatherings with friends and family.


And while there are ample video conferencing tools on the market today,
Zoom seems to be the one in the spotlight.
Headquartered in San Jose and founded in 2011,
Zoom now sits at the top of similar videoconferencing platforms in the US and in Europe.
As our economy fades, Zoom has seen its company rise to $29 billion in valuation.


In this less-than-ideal situation,
we get a sense of how social networking platforms
become essential in our daily-to-day communications, for our social life.
We use it to connect to our favorite peoples
as the world fights to contain the virus.

So how does one have a fun Zoom night?
